Karolina Dziadura, PhD

Postdoctoral Researcher at the Minor Planet Center, Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian

I am an astrophysicist working on asteroid orbits, celestial mechanics, and astrometric data analysis. I use Gaia space mission data and ground-based observations to study the Yarkovsky effect, photometric center displacement, and orbit refinement.

Email: karolina.dziadura@cfa.harvard.edu

Research

My research focuses on the Yarkovsky effect, photocenter-to-barycenter offset, and the precision of asteroid orbits using Gaia astrometry, photometry, and orbit determination techniques (e.g., OrbFit).

Selected Publications:

  • Dziadura et al. 2025 – A&A – Detection of the Yarkovsky effect with Gaia DR3 and FPR
  • Dziadura et al. 2023 – A&A – Asteroid density estimation with Gaia data
  • Dziadura et al. 2022 – Icarus – Yarkovsky candidates from Gaia DR2
  • Leśniewska et al. 2022 – ApJS – GRBs and ISM studies
  • Podlewska-Gaca et al. 2020 – A&A – Mass parameter estimation
