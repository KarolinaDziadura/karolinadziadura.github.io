Karolina Dziadura, PhD

Astrophysicist at Adam Mickiewicz University, Faculty of Physics and Astronomy, Poznań, Poland
Collaborating with the Minor Planet Center at the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian, Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA

I am an astrophysicist working on asteroid orbits, celestial mechanics, and astrometric data analysis. My research uses Gaia data and ground-based observations to investigate the Yarkovsky effect, improve asteroid orbits, and study photocenter–barycenter offsets.

I am currently conducting a statistical analysis of observatory performance to develop a comprehensive weighting scheme for automated and improved orbit determination.

In recognition of my scientific and outreach activities, the minor planet (20446) Karolinadziadura was named in my honor. Its orbital parameters are available in the NASA/JPL Small-Body Database .

Research

My main research interests include:

  • Statistical analysis of astrometric residuals
  • Yarkovsky effect detection and modeling
  • Photocenter–barycenter offset analysis
  • Asteroid orbit determination and refinement
  • Long-arc astrometry and observatory performance
